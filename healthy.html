Healthy Eating Hub

Healthy Eating & Nutrition Learning Hub

This interactive learning space helps users understand nutrition fundamentals through structured guides, key concepts and practical dietary knowledge. Click each card to expand and explore detailed nutrition information.

Macronutrients

  • Protein: muscle repair, growth, enzyme production
  • Carbohydrates: main energy source for brain + body
  • Fats: hormone regulation + long-term energy storage

Micronutrients

  • Vitamins: support immunity and metabolic function
  • Minerals: bone health, oxygen transport, nerve function
  • Examples: iron, calcium, magnesium, vitamin C

Hydration

  • Supports energy levels and focus
  • Helps digestion and nutrient absorption
  • Aim: ~6–8 cups daily (more if active)

Balanced Plate Method

  • ½ plate: vegetables or salad
  • ¼ plate: lean protein
  • ¼ plate: complex carbohydrates
  • Helps maintain balanced energy intake

Food Groups

  • Fruits & vegetables: vitamins + fibre
  • Grains: energy source (prefer whole grains)
  • Protein foods: repair and growth
  • Dairy or alternatives: calcium + bone health

Healthy Eating Principles

  • Eat mostly whole, unprocessed foods
  • Reduce added sugar and ultra-processed foods
  • Maintain consistent meal timing
  • Prioritise fibre-rich foods daily

Reading Food Labels

  • Check serving size first
  • Look at sugar and sodium levels
  • Compare saturated fat per 100g
  • Ingredients listed from highest to lowest

Common Myths

  • Carbs do NOT automatically cause weight gain
  • Fat is not inherently unhealthy
  • “Detox diets” are unnecessary for health
  • Balance matters more than restriction

Energy Balance

  • Energy in = calories consumed
  • Energy out = activity + metabolism
  • Weight changes depend on long-term balance

Fibre Importance

  • Improves digestion and gut health
  • Helps maintain stable blood sugar
  • Found in fruit, vegetables, grains, legumes
