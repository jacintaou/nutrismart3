What is Healthy Eating?

Healthy eating is about consuming a balanced diet that provides your body with the nutrients it needs to function properly. It is not about restriction, but about balance, variety and moderation.

  • Focus on whole, minimally processed foods
  • Maintain a balance of all food groups
  • Ensure adequate hydration daily
  • Support long-term physical and mental health

Macronutrients

Macronutrients are nutrients the body needs in large amounts to provide energy and support growth and repair.

  • Protein: builds and repairs muscles and tissues
  • Carbohydrates: main source of energy for the body
  • Fats: support hormones and brain function

Micronutrients

Micronutrients are essential vitamins and minerals required in small amounts but are critical for overall health.

  • Support immune system function
  • Help energy production and metabolism
  • Maintain bone, skin and organ health
  • Examples: iron, calcium, vitamin C

Hydration

Water is essential for nearly every function in the body, including digestion, circulation and temperature control.

  • Improves concentration and energy levels
  • Supports digestion and nutrient absorption
  • Recommended: 6–8 cups daily (more if active)

Balanced Plate

The balanced plate method helps create meals that include the right proportions of nutrients.

  • ½ vegetables or salad for fibre and vitamins
  • ¼ lean protein for growth and repair
  • ¼ carbohydrates for energy

Food Groups

Foods are grouped based on their nutritional role in the body to help guide balanced eating.

  • Fruits & vegetables → vitamins, minerals, fibre
  • Grains → energy (prefer whole grains)
  • Protein foods → growth and repair
  • Dairy → calcium for bones

Food Labels

Food labels provide important nutritional information that helps consumers make informed choices.

  • Check serving size first
  • Monitor sugar and sodium content
  • Compare values per 100g
  • Ingredients are listed from highest to lowest

Nutrition Myths

Many common beliefs about food are misleading or incorrect. Understanding facts helps improve decision-making.

  • Carbohydrates do NOT cause weight gain alone
  • Fats are essential for health in moderation
  • Detox diets are not scientifically necessary

Energy Balance

Energy balance is the relationship between calories consumed and calories used by the body.

  • Weight gain occurs when intake > output
  • Weight loss occurs when output > intake
  • Maintenance = balanced energy levels

Fibre

Fibre is a type of carbohydrate that supports digestive health and long-term wellbeing.

  • Improves gut health and digestion
  • Helps regulate blood sugar levels
  • Found in fruits, vegetables and grains
